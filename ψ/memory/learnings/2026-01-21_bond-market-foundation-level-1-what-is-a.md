# ## Bond Market Foundation - Level 1

## Bond Market Foundation - Level 1

### What is a Bond?
A bond is a debt instrument where investor lends money to issuer (government/corporation) in exchange for periodic interest payments and return of principal at maturity.

### Bond Components
| Component | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Face Value (Par) | Principal amount | $1,000 |
| Coupon Rate | Annual interest rate | 5% |
| Maturity | Time until repayment | 10 years |
| Yield | Actual return based on price | 4.5% |
| Price | Market trading price | $950 or $1,050 |

### Bond Types
**Government Bonds:**
- US Treasury (lowest risk, benchmark)
- JGB Japan (low risk)
- Thai Gov Bond (low-medium)
- Emerging market sovereign (higher risk)

**Corporate Bonds:**
- AAA (Apple, Microsoft) - 4-5% yield
- BBB (Investment grade) - 5-7% yield
- BB/Junk (Speculative) - 8-12% yield
- CCC (Near default) - 15%+ yield

### US Treasury Types
- T-Bills: < 1 year, no coupon, least volatile
- T-Notes: 2-10 years, with coupon
- T-Bonds: 20-30 years, most volatile
- TIPS: Inflation protected
